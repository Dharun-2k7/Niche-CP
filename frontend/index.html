About the ICPC Club

The Amrita Nagercoil ICPC Club is a student-led organization dedicated to cultivating a rigorous algorithmic problem-solving culture on campus. NicheCP is our custom-built platform designed to support our members through consistent practice, organized events, and measurable progress.

Building Culture

Establishing a campus environment where algorithmic problem-solving is a regular, collaborative, and respected activity.

Regular Participation

Providing the technical infrastructure to host weekly contests, ensuring students have consistent opportunities to compete.

ICPC Preparation

Simulating the pressure, formats, and difficulty levels of the International Collegiate Programming Contest to prepare campus teams.

Contest Categories

Standard format consisting of multiple algorithmic problems of increasing difficulty. Ranked by problems solved and time penalties.

Code Golf

The ICPC Journey

The standard progression of a club member, from joining the community to representing the college at regional and international competitions.

1

Join the Club

Register on NicheCP, explore the problem archive, and integrate into the Amrita Nagercoil coding community.

Attend Weekly Contests

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior competitive programming experience to join?

No. The club and platform support students at all levels. We recommend starting with the problem archive and attending editorial sessions to build foundational knowledge.

How are ratings calculated?

NicheCP utilizes an ELO-based rating system, similar to Codeforces. Your rating adjusts after every official contest based on your relative performance against other participants.

How do I form an ICPC team?

Teams consist of three members. You can form a team organically during club meetings or participate in our team-building events early in the semester. Teams must register through the official ICPC portal.

Can I contribute problems to the platform?

Yes. Experienced members can apply for Problem Setter access to draft, test, and deploy new algorithmic challenges for upcoming contests.
